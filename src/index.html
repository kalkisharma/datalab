Parity statistics

NSE — Nash–Sutcliffe efficiency: 1 − Σ(modelled−observed)² / Σ(observed−mean of observed)². 1 = perfect agreement; 0 = the model predicts no better than the observed mean; below 0 = worse than the mean.

MAE — mean absolute error: the average |modelled − observed|, in data units.

RMSE — root-mean-square error: √(mean of (modelled−observed)²). Penalizes large misses more heavily than MAE.
